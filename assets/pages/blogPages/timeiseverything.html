Time is Everything

In physics

, we learn that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. An induced current will produce a magnetic field in the opposite direction. These laws of conservation are found everywhere in the physical world and one of the coolest things about this is that it doesn't take a physicist to understand this. This physical observation is found in "non-physical" scenes as well. For instance, taking that last slice of pizza (action) just means that someone else won't get it (reaction). Forgetting to buy a present for your friend's birthday means that you'll have one less friend (Just kidding, hopefully that doesn't happen).

But here's the catch.

In the examples above, time was not an important/emphasized variable. Sure you could take the list slice of pizza, but you can just buy another one. And sure you forgot it was your friend's birthday, but you can always just get them a late gift. This is important because time is the one variable in all of the universe that is not conserved. To get an idea of what this means, image yourself just sitting down on the floor doing absolutely nothing. As you are sitting there, time is ticking down, but nothing is happening. You aren't getting any healthier and you're not learning anything. You are wasting time.

I find this to be a significant observation

because it means that we have to make meaningful decisions everyday in order to become successful. Optimizing our day-to-day schedule is part of this decision process. We have to spend less time doing things that don't benefit you, like playing LoL, and more time doing things that matter such as side projects or working on becoming a better person.
